Introduction & History

Writing System: In the Japanese Language there are 3 writing systems that are used together to form the language. They are Hiragana, Katakana, and Kanji. Hiragana can be considered the backbone of the language. Hiragana is used to represent words that are Japanese Origin. All of the Katakana words sound exactly the same as those in hiragana but the major diffrence is that it is associated with foreign words(that don’t originate from Japan). For example Soda would be written in katakana as ソーダ(So-Da) not そーだ (hiragana). The closest and proper way to write soda in hiragana would be written as 炭酸飲料 (Tansan inryou / たんさんいんりょう). If you were to break down the word it would literally mean “Carbonated Drink”.
